<p>I was sitting in a Greek theater watching a play together with several other people. It was a lucid dream, but I let it unfold on its own and allowed my subconscious to generate it. (I sometimes do this when I feel adventurous, or when it feels like that changing things would destabilize the dream.)</p>

<p>The audience around me in the theater were very enthusiastic about the performance. They seemed moved by it and kept congratulating, and thanking me for writing such a good play.</p>

<p>At first I was confused because I didn’t remember writing any play. Then I realized that since this was my dream, the play was being generated by my mind in real time. In that sense I must have had something to do with creating it, but it still felt awkward to claim authorship.</p>

<p>It also felt strange that the dream characters were the ones attributing the authorship to me. I assumed they were generated by the same subconscious process that was generating the play itself, yet within the dream they still reacted to it as if I were its author.</p>
